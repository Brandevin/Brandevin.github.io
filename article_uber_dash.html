Visualizing Uber Rides on NY using Python's Dash

In this project, a dashboard to visualize Uber rides in New York was created. The data was obtained from Kaggle and contains information about pickup and dropoff location, datetime of the ride and number of passengers. It contains 200.000 rides that happened in New York between january 2009 and june 2015. A subset of the data is shown in Table 1.

Table 1. Kaggle original dataset

To study and group the data, New York was divided into more than 2000 quadrants, each with 0.005 degrees of longitude and 0.005 degrees of latitude, as shown in Table 2. Each pickup and dropoff location was assigned to a specific location, as shown in Table 3.

Table 2. Maximum latitudes and longitudes of quadrants

Table 3. Processed dataset, with information about quadrant of origin and destination

A dashboard with the data was created using Python's library "dash" and published to Heroku. It allows the user to explore the following questions:

  • What are the most common destination of people that get an Uber in a quadrant? What is the most common origin of people that arrive at the quadrant?
  • How do the pickups and dropoffs at a quadrant behave along the day? Are they constant during the day or more common during morning/afternoon?
  • How has the number of rides changed over the years?
  • Inside the quadrant, what are the most common streets that people get an Uber?
