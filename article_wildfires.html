Visualizing USA historical wildfires with Tableau Dashboard

Wildfires are unplanned, unwanted or uncontrolled fires in areas of combustile vegetation. In the United States, the national Fire Program Analysis (FPA) system is responsible for acquiring reports from federal, state and local fire organizations. The maps below shows some of the most fire-susceptible regions of the US as well as the National Forests of the United states.

Wildland hazard potential (WHP) map produced by the USDA Forest Service, Fire Modeling Institute. Uses estimates of burn probability and conditional probabilities of fire intensity levels to build a Large Fire Simulator. The higher the WFP, the higher the potential of a high-intensity fire.*

USA National Forests Map.*

The data was acquired, processed, filtered and finally made available in Kaggle for anyone to analyze. It contains 1.88 Million wildfire records registered between 1992 and 2015, representing a total of 140 Million acres burned in the period.

The data includes records of root cause, fire size, discovery and containment dates, owner of land, name of the reporting unit.

A dashboard with this data was created and is shown above (as well as accessible through the buttom below) and is interactable: One can filter the cause of fire, year of fire and fire size. One can also use the mouse to select the area of interest. The filtered region properties are then displayed in the three barplots below, where one can understand the main fire causes, the regions most susceptible to fires, the behaviour over time, the main reporting units in region and much more.

By using the dashboard, one can quickly reach the following conclusions:

  • Lightings were the main cause of large fires. They occurred mainly in the region between the Rocky Mountains and the pacific, as well as in Alaska;
  • Most railroad fires occurred in Florida;
  • The states of Iowa, Illionois, Indiana and Ohio presented a low number of fires. Most likely because they are very agricultural regions.
  • There is a very high correlation between fire size and density of fires verified in the dashboard and the WHP map. This is expected, as the Large Fire Simulator was built using this same database.
  • The majority os fires in Puerto Rico and Hawaii had no cause reported.
  • If one wants to better understand the fires that occurred in Idaho, one should contact Boise Distric, Twin Falls District, Idaho Falls Distric, Nez Perce - Clearwater National Forests, Salmon-Challis National Forest. On the other hand, if one wants to know more about Florida fires, one should contact the Florida Forest Service
  • The total area of fires seem to have increased in Kansas over time.
