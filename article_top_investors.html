Creating Buy/Sell strategies based on top investors behaviour

The SEC's 13F is a quarterly report required to be filed by institutional managers that manage at least 100$ million. It discloses their equity holdings and can provide insights into what the top managers are doing. It informs what was bought and sold in the past quarter and must be filed until the filings date, 45 days after the end of the quarter.

The objective of this analysis will be to explore if, by considering the actions of multiple funds (and trying to find which stocks they seem to agree on), it is possible to find stocks that are likely to perform better than the S&P in the period after the filings date.

Along the analysis, the following topics will be explored:

  • Do managers that performed well after the filings date in the past perform well in the future? Funds with more stocks perform better than funds with less stocks? Funds with a lower capital amount perform better?
  • How should we filter the data to include only relevant fund transactions?
  • How could we build strategies based on the top funds behavior? Which performs better?
  • How sensitive is the performance of the methods to change in their parameters?
